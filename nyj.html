BORN A CRIME

By: Trevoh Noah

Chapter 1


Chapter 1 provides an overview of the historical context in South Africa before and during apartheid,
focusing on the rivalry between the Zulu and Xhosa tribes. It highlights the impact of apartheid on the country's people,
with particular emphasis on the experiences of Nelson Mandela, who was Xhosa like Trevor Noah's mother.
Trevor Noah, the author, introduces himself as a product of apartheid, born in 1984, and presents anecdotes from
his childhood that illustrate the complexities of living in a racially segregated society.
The chapter delves into Trevor's upbringing within a devout Christian family, where attending church was a
significant part of their weekly routine. Despite the logistical challenges of getting to various churches,
Trevor's mother's unwavering faith in God is highlighted, along with an incident
where she bravely confronts a menacing bus driver, ultimately saving Trevor's life.

Chapter 2

Chapter 2 delves deeper into the apartheid regime, tracing its origins back to Dutch colonizers in the 17th century,
its formalization by the Afrikaners after British rule ended.
Trevor shares the story of his birth, being born to a Black mother and a white Swiss father, which was illegal under apartheid laws.
His mother's courage in navigating the complexities of interracial relationships,
including seeking sanctuary with a Swiss friend in Johannesburg to give birth, is portrayed.
Trevor's existence was against the law, leading to secrecy and fear for his family's safety, with his mother having to evade questions about his light skin.
The chapter also highlights the harsh realities faced by mixed-race individuals under apartheid, including the constant threat of being taken by the police,
as well as the difficult choices faced by Trevor's mother, who chose to stay in South Africa despite the risks.
Through these anecdotes, Trevor Noah provides a personal and poignant insight into the intricacies of life in apartheid-era South Africa,
setting the stage for further exploration of his childhood experiences in subsequent chapters.
